# BDM Project

## Title
**An Analytical Study of Demand Patterns and Price Ambiguity in a Construction Materials Retail Store**

---

## Project Overview
This Business Data Management (BDM) project focuses on analyzing sales, demand variability, pricing ambiguity, and inventory behavior in a construction materials retail store. The project uses statistical analysis, visualization, and business insights to identify operational inefficiencies and recommend improvements for inventory and pricing management.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze demand patterns of construction materials
- Identify products with unstable pricing
- Detect stockout situations and inventory issues
- Study sales trends and purchasing behavior
- Generate business recommendations using data analysis

---

## Dataset Information
The dataset contains transaction and inventory details of construction materials such as:
- Cement
- TMT Bars
- Stirrups
- Aggregate materials

### Dataset Features
- Product Name
- Purchase Quantity
- Sales Quantity
- Opening Stock
- Closing Stock
- Purchase Price
- Selling Price
- Revenue
- Demand Trends

---

## Tools and Technologies Used
- Python

- Statistical Analysis Techniques

---

## Analysis Performed

### 1. Data Cleaning and Preprocessing
- Removed missing and duplicate values
- Corrected inconsistent entries
- Standardized numerical data
- Prepared dataset for analysis

### 2. Descriptive Statistics
Calculated:
- Mean
- Median
- Standard Deviation
- Variance
- Skewness

### 3. Demand Analysis
- Monthly demand trends
- Product-wise demand fluctuations
- Seasonal demand behavior

### 4. Pricing Analysis
- Price ambiguity identification
- Comparison of selling and purchase prices
- Detection of inconsistent pricing patterns

### 5. Inventory Analysis
- Opening vs Closing stock analysis
- Identification of stockout situations
- Overstock and understock observations

### 6. Visualization
Created graphs such as:
- Bar Charts
- Line Charts
- Boxplots
- Histograms
- Revenue Trend Graphs

---

## Key Findings
- Cement and stirrup products showed significant pricing ambiguity.
- TMT bars experienced frequent stockouts due to reactive purchasing.
- Demand for cement and TMT bars was highly variable.
- Some products showed inconsistent inventory management practices.
- Sales patterns indicated opportunities for better forecasting and stock planning.

---

## Recommendations
- Implement demand forecasting techniques
- Maintain safety stock for high-demand products
- Standardize pricing policies
- Improve inventory monitoring systems
- Use data-driven purchasing strategies




---

## Conclusion
The project successfully identified operational inefficiencies in pricing and inventory management within a construction materials retail store. Using data analysis and visualization techniques, the study provided actionable recommendations to improve business decision-making and inventory planning.
